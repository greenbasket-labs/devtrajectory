# DevTrajectory  
**Builder signals over time, not resumes.**

DevTrajectory is a research-driven framework for observing how developers grow through real work, public contribution, and impact â€” rather than job titles, years of experience, or social clout.

This project explores a simple idea:  
> Developer value is not static. It moves.

---

## Problem

Today, developers are commonly evaluated using:
- Years of experience
- Job titles
- Company logos
- Online visibility

These signals often fail to reflect:
- Actual shipped work
- Real users served
- Open-source contribution quality
- Growth over time

As a result:
- Junior developers with real impact are overlooked  
- Senior developers with low output remain overvalued  
- Builders are incentivized to signal status instead of building useful things  

---

## Core Idea

**DevTrajectory** introduces a *trajectory-based* way of thinking about developer value.

Instead of ranking developers absolutely, it focuses on **movement over time**, similar to how markets observe price trends rather than labels.

> Think charts, not titles.

The goal is not to judge â€” but to **observe growth**.

## Builder Signals (Conceptual)

DevTrajectory considers **signals**, not credentials.  
Examples include:

- Public deployments (testnet or mainnet)
- Open-source contributions
- Hackathon submissions
- Grant milestones
- Documentation written
- Tools or services used by real people
- Research artifacts shared publicly

No single signal dominates.  
Signals compound over time.

---

## Trajectory Model (Illustrative)

Instead of ranks, developers may be described as:

- **Emerging** â€” early public work, learning in the open  
- **Growing** â€” consistent output and experimentation  
- **Proven** â€” sustained delivery and community value  
- **Sustained** â€” long-term contribution and reliability  

These are **descriptive**, not competitive.

## Why This Matters

- Rewards real building, not noise
- Protects junior developers from being dismissed unfairly
- Keeps senior developers accountable through output
- Encourages long-term contribution over short-term hype
- Aligns with Web3 values: openness, verifiability, permissionless growth

A junior developer with one meaningful, widely used project can outperform a senior developer with years of inactivity â€” and that should be visible.
